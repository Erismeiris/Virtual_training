Annual Teaching Plan

PROPERTIES OF 2D SHAPES Grade 6

Term Topic
Term 3
  • Recognize, visualize and name 2-D shapes in the environment and geometric settings
    Regular and irregular polygons, triangles, squares, rectangles, parallelograms, other quadrilaterals, pentagons, hexagons, heptagons and octagons
  • Similarities and differences between rectangles and parallelograms
  • Features of shapes:
    • Describe, sort and compare 2-D shapes in terms of:
      • number of sides
      • length of sides
      • size of angles:
        1. acute
        2. right
        3. obtuse
        4. straight
        5. reflex
        6. revolution
  • Draw 2D shapes on grid paper
  • Further activities :
    • Draw circles, patterns in circles and patterns with circles using a pair of compasses.

Practical activity

Use the OpenBoard software to find the Similarities and Differences between parallelograms and rectangles, taking into account the number of sides, length of sides, sizes of angles.

Criteria Similarities Differences
Number of sides Both have 4 sides
Length of sides Both has 2 pairs of equal sides
Size of angles Rectangles has 4 right angles and Parallelogram angles are not necessarily right angles.
